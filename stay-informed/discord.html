Connect with advocates in your district.

Our Discord server has a dedicated channel for every school district. Find the people organizing near you, share what you know, and coordinate action — all in a space with clear rules to protect members.

Discord is for local organizing and discussion. For general news and updates, follow us on Instagram. When it is time to take a specific action, we send an email.

Rules and moderation

The server has clear community rules. Moderators enforce them. Members who violate the rules lose access.

Getting started takes about 30 seconds

1
Read and agree to the rules

Go to #rules and react with 👍. You'll need to do this before joining any discussions.

2
Choose your state

Go to #choose-your-state and click your state's emoji to unlock your state's channels.

3

In the choose-your-district-[state] thread, click your district's emoji to join local discussions. Not listed? Post in #request-a-district.

Community rules

Protect privacy

Never share or request immigration status, addresses, phone numbers, or any identifying details about students or families. When in doubt, share less.

Be respectful

No harassment, threats, hate speech, or demeaning language. Keep discussions constructive.

Stay focused

This space is for school policies, local organizing, and useful resources. Avoid off-topic debates, spam, or unrelated politics.

No misinformation

Do not share unverified claims. Include sources when possible and clearly label uncertain information.

Respect anonymity

Do not pressure others to reveal their identity or move conversations off-platform in ways that could compromise safety.

If you see a violation, do not engage — report it to a moderator. Violations may result in a warning, loss of access, or removal from the server.
