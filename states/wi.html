Overview

State context

Wisconsin has limited state-level immigration enforcement protections. Milwaukee has taken a more welcoming local posture, and Milwaukee Public Schools serves a significant immigrant population. ICE is active in the Milwaukee metro area and in agricultural communities. The state government has generally been unsupportive of formal protective policies.

ICE activity

ICE is active in Wisconsin, particularly in the Milwaukee metro area, in Green Bay, and in the dairy farming communities of central and northern Wisconsin. Wisconsin's dairy industry relies heavily on undocumented immigrant workers.

School policies

Milwaukee Public Schools has informally worked to support immigrant families, but no formal warrant-based policy is in place. Madison schools have been more proactive about welcoming practices. The state's lack of formal protections leaves most districts without guidance.

Notes for advocates

Wisconsin's dairy industry depends extensively on undocumented immigrant labor, creating large immigrant communities in rural counties that have few formal advocacy resources. Milwaukee has a significant Mexican and Central American community. Advocates have focused on legal services and rapid response given the lack of formal policy protections.
