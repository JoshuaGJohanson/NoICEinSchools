How to Speak at a Davis School District Board Meeting

Speaking at a board meeting is one of the most direct and effective ways to influence district policy. Board members pay attention to constituents who show up and make their voices heard. This guide walks you through everything you need to know to participate with confidence.

You do not need to be part of an organization to speak. Anyone can participate.

When and where meetings happen

Location

AddressDavis School District Office
Farmington, Utah

Typical schedule

Regular Meeting~6:00 PM (includes public comment period)

Check the district website to confirm times before attending — schedules can vary.

Agendas and materials

  • Agendas are posted in advance at meetings.boardbook.org/public/Organization/914
  • Review the agenda before attending — it shows what topics will be discussed and when public comment is scheduled
  • Board meeting materials are also available through the same link
Tip: The board can only take action on items on the agenda. Public comment is your opportunity to raise issues that are not yet on the agenda — and to signal that a topic deserves future attention.

How the meeting is structured

Understanding the meeting format helps you know where to be and when to speak. Most Davis School District board meetings follow this sequence:

  1. Opening items — Routine business including roll call, approval of minutes, and consent items. You do not need to be present for this portion.
  2. Public comment period — The designated time for community members to address the board directly. This is your window. Sign in before the meeting starts to ensure your name is on the list.
  3. Board discussion and decisions — The board deliberates on agenda items and takes formal action. Public comment is typically complete before this portion.
Important: Public comment is a listening period for the board. Members will typically not respond directly to your comment during the meeting — that is normal, not a sign you were ignored. Your comment is on the record and can influence future agenda items.

How to sign up to speak

At the meeting

Sign-up typically happens in person before the meeting begins. Arrive early — at least 15–20 minutes before the meeting starts — to add your name to the public comment list.

Depending on the agenda, sign-up may also be available in advance. Check the posted agenda or contact the district office if you want to confirm the process before attending.

What to expect at sign-in

You may be asked to provide:

  • Your name
  • The general topic of your comment

You do not need special experience or credentials to speak. If you live, work, or have a child in Davis School District, your voice belongs in this conversation.

Time limits and expectations

Per speaker

2–3 minutes

Each speaker typically receives 2 to 3 minutes. The board may adjust this based on the number of speakers. Practice your comment so you can deliver it clearly within the limit.

Total comment period

Limited

Total public comment time is limited. If many people sign up, the board may reduce individual time to accommodate everyone. Arriving early and signing in promptly helps secure your spot.

Conduct expectations

  • Stay on topic
  • Be respectful and professional
  • No personal attacks on board members, staff, or other speakers
  • Be concise — say your key point early
  • Yield the floor when your time is up
Practice out loud. Two to three minutes is shorter than it sounds. A timed practice run before the meeting will help you stay focused and calm at the podium.

Optional: Connecting with others

Some people coordinate with others before attending. Others come on their own. Both approaches work — and both matter.

Whether you attend alone or with others, your voice counts. A single thoughtful comment from a constituent can influence a board member. Multiple speakers across multiple meetings signal sustained community commitment.

If you are attending alone

  • Pick one clear angle from the focus categories above
  • Practice your comment out loud before the meeting
  • Arrive early and sign in for public comment
  • Consider returning to future meetings — consistency matters

If you are coordinating with others

  • Assign each speaker a different angle so comments build on each other
  • Agree on the specific ask so all speakers are aligned
  • Sit together as a visible group — presence matters even for those not speaking
  • Debrief after and plan your next appearance

After the meeting

  • Follow up with board members by email to reinforce your message
  • Plan to return — issues raised repeatedly are harder to ignore
  • Share this page with others who care about this issue
