Weber School District · Utah

Weber School District: No ICE in Schools

Clear procedures, trained staff, and consistent responses across all schools in the district.

Sign Up for Updates Contact the School Board ← Utah Overview
● No written policy
● No standardized staff training
● No family communication protocol

What we are asking for

  • Require a judge-signed warrant before immigration enforcement officers enter non-public school areas or remove a student
  • Train front office staff with a written protocol and clear escalation path
  • Notify families promptly when enforcement activity affects the school or a student

Why it matters

  • Staff placed in high-pressure enforcement situations need clear, written procedures — not improvised responses
  • Consistency across schools is critical: families should receive the same answer regardless of which campus their child attends
  • Families need accurate, reliable information about what the district will and will not do
  • Weber's size and geographic spread make a standardized districtwide response especially important

What is happening now

  • Weber School District has no clearly communicated, district-wide protocol for how schools should respond to immigration enforcement visits
  • How a school responds depends on which administrator is available — responses may vary significantly across the district's many campuses
  • Front office staff have no documented procedure for what to do, who to call, or what they are and are not required to allow
  • Families have no clear information about what the district would do if officers arrived at their child's school
  • Weber communities — including Ogden's established immigrant and refugee populations — deserve a clear, written commitment from the district

Why does this matter specifically in Weber?

Weber School District serves communities across Weber County — including Ogden, where established immigrant and refugee communities have been a part of the city for generations. These families are directly affected by immigration enforcement activity.

  • Ogden's Hispanic/Latino community represents a significant share of Weber District enrollment, with many families navigating mixed immigration status
  • Weber County's enforcement environment means that local and federal agencies are both active — families need to know what protections exist inside schools
  • Without a consistent policy, how a school responds depends on which administrator answers the door — creating real uncertainty for families across a large, geographically spread district
