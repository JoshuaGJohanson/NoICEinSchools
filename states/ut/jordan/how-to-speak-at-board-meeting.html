How to Speak at a Jordan School District Board Meeting

Speaking at a board meeting is one of the most direct and effective ways to influence district policy. Board members are elected representatives — they pay attention to constituents who show up. This guide walks you through everything you need to know to participate with confidence.

When and where meetings happen

Location

AddressJATC South Campus
12723 S Park Avenue
Riverton, Utah

Typical schedule

Study Session~4:00 PM (discussion; public comment not always available)
Regular Meeting~6:30 PM (public portion, includes Patron Comment)

Check the district website to confirm times before attending — schedules can vary.

Online access and agendas

  • Meetings are livestreamed at boardmeeting.jordandistrict.org
  • Agendas are posted ahead of time at jordandistrict.org/board/meetings/
  • Review the agenda before attending — it tells you what topics will be discussed and when Patron Comment is scheduled
Tip: The board can only take action on items on the agenda. Patron Comment is your opportunity to raise issues that are not on the agenda — and to signal that a topic deserves future attention.

How the meeting is structured

Understanding the meeting format helps you know where to be and when to be there. Most Jordan School District board meetings follow this sequence:

  1. Closed session — Not open to the public. The board meets privately to discuss personnel, legal, or other confidential matters. You do not need to attend for this portion.
  2. Study session (~4:00 PM) — A working discussion session. Sometimes public comment is not available during this portion. Arrive and observe even if you cannot speak.
  3. Regular meeting (~6:30 PM) — The formal public meeting. This is where Patron Comment happens and where your participation matters most.
  4. Patron Comment — The designated period when members of the public can address the board directly. This is your window.
Important: The board cannot vote or take action on items not listed on the agenda. Your goal during Patron Comment is to put an issue on the board's radar, demonstrate community concern, and build pressure for it to appear on a future agenda.

How to sign up to speak

At the meeting

Sign-up typically happens in person before the meeting begins. Arrive early — at least 15–20 minutes before the regular meeting starts — to add your name to the public comment list. Ask a staff member at the entrance where to sign in for Patron Comment.

Depending on the agenda and the number of speakers, sign-in procedures may vary. Check the agenda posted online before attending so you know what to expect.

Who gets priority

Per district policy, priority for public comment is given in this order:

  1. District residents
  2. Students enrolled in the district
  3. Parents of enrolled students
  4. District employees
  5. Others (if time remains)

If you live, work, or have a child in the Jordan School District, you are near the top of this list. Show up and sign in early.

Time limits and expectations

Per speaker

3 minutes

Each speaker receives up to 3 minutes. The board may shorten this if there are many speakers. Practice your comment so you can deliver it comfortably within the limit.

Total comment period

~45 minutes

The total Patron Comment period is typically capped at approximately 45 minutes. If many people sign up, the board may reduce individual time to accommodate everyone.

Conduct expectations

  • Stay on topic
  • Be respectful and professional
  • No personal attacks on board members, staff, or other speakers
  • Yield the floor when your time is up
Practice out loud. Three minutes is shorter than it sounds. A timed practice run before the meeting will help you stay focused and calm at the podium.

Coordinating with others

Organized, consistent participation across multiple meetings is one of the most effective ways to influence school board decisions. A single speaker is noted. Ten speakers across three meetings — each covering a different angle, each returning — signals a sustained community commitment that boards take seriously.

Before the meeting

  • Identify who is speaking and what angle each person will cover
  • Agree on the specific ask so all speakers are aligned
  • Practice your individual comments — out loud, timed
  • Arrive together and sign in early

During the meeting

  • Sit together as a visible group — presence matters even for those not speaking
  • Listen to other speakers so you can build on, not repeat, what has been said
  • Stay professional and calm regardless of how the board responds

After the meeting

  • Debrief as a group — what landed, what to adjust next time
  • Follow up with board members by email to reinforce your message
  • Plan your next meeting appearance before leaving
