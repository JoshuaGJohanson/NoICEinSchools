Nevada

A high-level overview of immigration enforcement activity, school protection policies, and resources for families and educators in Nevada.

Overview

State context

Nevada has enacted meaningful state-level protections. AB 281 (Nevada Trust Act, 2019) limits law enforcement cooperation with ICE civil immigration detainers. Las Vegas has a large immigrant community, and Clark County School District — the fifth largest in the country — serves tens of thousands of students from mixed-status families. The state has generally moved toward stronger protections in recent years.

ICE activity

ICE is active in Nevada, particularly in the Las Vegas metro area, which has a large undocumented population primarily from Latin America. Enforcement operations have targeted neighborhoods in Clark County.

School policies

Clark County School District has adopted policies aligned with the Nevada Trust Act, limiting staff cooperation with immigration enforcement. The district has issued guidance on responding to enforcement requests and maintaining family trust. The Nevada Department of Education has supported welcoming school practices.

Notes for advocates

Clark County School District's scale — nearly 330,000 students — means its policies have enormous impact. The Las Vegas construction and hospitality industries have historically employed many immigrant workers. Nevada's shift toward stronger protections reflects the growing political power of the state's Latino community.
