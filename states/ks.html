Overview

State context

Kansas has no state protections for schools regarding immigration enforcement. The state has a significant immigrant workforce in western Kansas meatpacking communities (Liberal, Garden City, Dodge City). ICE is active, particularly in southwest Kansas. The state government has generally been unsupportive of protective local policies.

ICE activity

ICE operations in Kansas focus heavily on western Kansas meatpacking communities. The Garden City/Liberal corridor has experienced recurring enforcement activity tied to the beef processing industry.

School policies

No statewide or widely adopted school protection policies. Garden City and Liberal schools serve large immigrant populations without formal policies. Some administrators have worked informally to maintain family trust.

Notes for advocates

Garden City, Liberal, and Dodge City have among the most diverse school populations in the country relative to their size, driven by decades of meatpacking industry recruitment. These schools serve students from many countries and face significant challenges in the absence of formal protections. Local advocates and school counselors play critical roles.
