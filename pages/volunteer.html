Get involved

Campaigns for protective school policies need people willing to do the work.

There are many ways to contribute, whether you have an hour a week or much more. Here are the areas where help is most useful.

Contact Us Take Action

Ways to contribute

Community outreach

Talk to neighbors, parents at your child's school, faith community members, and local organizations. Personal conversations are how campaigns grow. You do not need talking points — you need to be genuine about why you care.

Research and writing

Help research your district's current policies, identify board members, draft letters, prepare public comment remarks, or write summaries of the research for local audiences.

Translation and language access

Many families who would be most helped by protective school policies may not read English as their primary language. Translation of materials and interpretation at meetings can significantly expand who is included in advocacy work.

Social media and communications

Help amplify the message on social media, draft posts, create shareable content, or manage online community spaces. Clear communication helps build the broad public support that school boards respond to.

Meeting and event support

Help organize community meetings, coordinate transportation for board meetings, set up childcare so more parents can attend, or provide hospitality and logistics support for local advocacy events.

Supporting affected families

Some of the most important work is direct: connecting families with legal resources, know-your-rights information, and community support. If you have relevant skills or connections, this kind of direct support matters.

What we need most

Right now, the most useful contributions are:

  • People willing to attend and speak at school board meetings in their district
  • Help translating materials into Spanish, Portuguese, Haitian Creole, and other languages
  • Researchers who can help document district policies and campaign outcomes
  • Community connectors who can bring more voices into local campaigns
  • People who can help families find legal resources and know-your-rights information
Get in Touch

Whether you have an hour or a hundred hours to give, there is a role for you in this work.
